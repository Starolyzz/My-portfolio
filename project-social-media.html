The Brief

Two brands, two distinct visual worlds.

Each client came with a different starting point. R&B Tea Houston had an existing social presence, but the posts felt generic — there was no clear visual identity tying them together. My job was to bring coherence and personality to their feed.

The Claw Spot was at the startup stage. I came on early enough to help establish their visual language from the ground up — every design decision carried extra weight, setting the tone for everything that followed.

The Process

Pinterest-first, brand-always.

My process typically started on Pinterest — gathering references that matched the mood, colour palette, or season of the upcoming post before touching any design software. This kept the work visually current without losing each brand's own character.

Three principles guided every piece:

  • Colour palette logic — the palette had to make sense for both the brand and the specific product or theme being featured.
  • Typographic consistency — fonts were locked early for each brand and treated as non-negotiable.
  • Brand presence in every variation — even for one-off event posts, the core brand elements had to remain visible and recognisable.

The Outcome

Feeds that feel intentional.

Across both brands I delivered a steady volume of content covering product features, promotional events, holiday posts, and special activations — including collaborative car meet events for R&B Tea Houston.

What I'm most proud of is the creative latitude this work gave me. Designing for two distinct brands simultaneously pushed me to think more deliberately about visual differentiation.

Every piece started with the brand, ended with the brief, and found its own visual personality somewhere in between.
