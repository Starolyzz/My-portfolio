The Brief

Identity work for brands at the very beginning.

Across nine Houston businesses — spanning food, beverage, and beauty — I was brought in to create original marks that could carry each brand's identity forward. The range was wide: a traditional Vietnamese bánh mì café, a contemporary coffee house, a playful street food brand, a beauty studio. Each required a different approach to style, tone, and visual language, which meant context-switching deliberately rather than defaulting to a house style.

The Process

From Pinterest boards to vector marks.

Every logo started the same way — a research phase to understand the visual language that fits each brand's personality and audience, then straight into Illustrator to build fully scalable vector marks. Across the nine brands I worked in four distinct logo styles: wordmarks, icon/symbol marks, combination marks, and badge/emblem formats. Every mark was built to function across the full range of touchpoints — menus, signage, social profiles, and print.

The Outcome

Marks that give brands something to build on.

Final deliverables were provided as PNG files across the required formats. Working across nine different brands in close succession sharpened my ability to context-switch deliberately — to step into a new visual world with each project rather than defaulting to a single style. That's one of the most valuable skills a designer can develop for freelance work.

The best marks look inevitable in hindsight — simple enough to be memorable, considered enough to hold meaning.
