The Brief

One page. Every screen. Everything they needed to say.

Di An Pho needed a complete print menu designed from scratch before opening — everything on a single double-sided page, no foldouts. Alongside that, I designed a matching placemat to extend the brand to the table itself. For R&B Tea Houston, the brief shifted to screen: TV menus for in-store display, adapting the full drink menu into both horizontal and vertical layouts that could be read at a distance and updated across locations.

The Process

Making constraint work for the design.

For Di An Pho, I worked in InDesign with Illustrator for custom graphic elements — balancing modern grid-based typography with Vietnamese design heritage through illustrated face watermarks, traditional corner marks, and a "How to Eat Phở" section that made the menu feel like a cultural object, not just a list. For R&B Tea, the challenge was legibility at scale: integrating real product photography directly into the layout so customers could see exactly what they were ordering, across both horizontal and vertical screen formats.

The Outcome

Ready to print, ready to display.

The Di An Pho deliverables — a press-ready double-sided menu PDF and matching placemat — went straight to print for opening day. The R&B Tea TV menus are now in use across locations as in-store digital displays, in both horizontal and vertical configurations.

The single-page constraint pushed me toward solutions I wouldn't have found with unlimited space. Restrictions are a useful creative tool.
