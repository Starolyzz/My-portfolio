The Brief

A brand identity. From nothing. In one day.

The Claw Spot is a claw machine arcade venue in Houston — launching from scratch and needing a complete visual identity before opening. I was brought on to design the logo with one condition: done within 24 hours. It needed to feel fun, playful, and high-energy while still being polished enough to carry across signage, merchandise, social media, and every other touchpoint the brand would eventually need.

The Logo

The Process

Character-first, then system.

With only 24 hours, I started with a clear concept: a badge emblem built around a kawaii mascot — a cheerful claw machine character with big sparkly eyes, rosy cheeks, and mechanical arms. The circular badge format referenced arcade tokens, with "THE" arching across the top and "CLAW SPOT" in bubbly inflated lettering across the bottom. The palette — periwinkle blue, coral pink, and gold — was chosen for maximum visual energy while staying cohesive.

The Outcome

The mark that started everything.

Delivered on time, the logo became the visual anchor for all of The Claw Spot's subsequent branding — grand opening materials, hiring posts, and the social media presence I went on to design. It was then manufactured as a backlit sign installed in the venue itself, glowing on the machines and the wall. What makes it significant isn't just the outcome, it's what it demonstrates: strong instincts and fast execution under real pressure.

The 24-hour deadline forced clarity. There was no time for second-guessing — only for making the best decision quickly and executing it completely.
